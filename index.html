Italy and UNESCO World Heritage Sites – The Aeolian Islands

Source: The Italian Tribune

With 59 locations and more that have been announced, Italy leads the world in the number of inscribed UNESCO sites. In this issue, we cover the thirty third Italian location to be included in the highly prestigious list.

In 2000, Isole Eolie or the Aeolian Islands were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. There are seven main islands, all volcanic in origin, forming an archipelago in the Tyrrhenian Sea north of Sicily. The islands offer a remarkable blend of scientific significance and rugged beauty. From Stromboli’s lava flow to the luxurious tranquility of Panarea, the Aeolian Islands showcase both geological legacy and Mediterranean charm.
